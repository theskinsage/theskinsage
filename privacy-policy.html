Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025 · The Skin Sage · theskinsage.com

This Privacy Policy explains how The Skin Sage collects, uses, stores and protects your personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Who we are

The Skin Sage is a virtual skin consultation service operated by its founder. We are the data controller for all personal data collected through this website and our consultation service.

Contact: hello@theskinsage.com

Website: theskinsage.com

What data we collect

We collect personal data that you provide to us directly when you use our services. This includes:

  • Contact information — the name you provide and your email address
  • Skin and health information provided through our intake form — including skin type, skin concerns, medical history relevant to skin health, lifestyle information and current skincare routine
  • Photographs of your face that you submit for consultation purposes
  • Communications between you and The Skin Sage by email

We do not collect payment information directly. Payment processing is handled by our third party payment provider and is subject to their privacy policy.

Why we collect your data and our legal basis

We collect and process your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To provide your consultation service — processing your intake form and photographs to produce your consultation report. Our legal basis is the performance of a contract with you.
  • To communicate with you about your consultation, your report and any follow up. Our legal basis is the performance of a contract with you.
  • To improve our service — understanding common skin concerns and improving our assessment methodology. Data used for this purpose is anonymised. Our legal basis is our legitimate interests.

We collect health-related information as part of our consultation service. This constitutes special category data under UK GDPR. Our legal basis for processing this data is your explicit consent, which you provide when you submit our intake form.

Your photographs

The photographs you submit are used solely for the purpose of producing your consultation report. They are not shared with any third party, not used in any marketing or promotional material, not published anywhere and not used to train any artificial intelligence system. Your photographs are deleted from our systems when your consultation is complete and your report has been delivered, unless you have requested a follow up service that requires them to be retained.

How we store your data

Your personal data is stored securely. We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction or alteration. We do not store your data on unsecured systems or share it with unauthorised parties.

How long we keep your data

We retain your personal data for as long as is necessary to provide our service to you and to comply with our legal obligations. Consultation records including intake form data are retained for a period of two years from the date of your consultation. You may request deletion of your data at any time — see Your Rights below.

Who we share your data with

We do not sell your personal data to any third party. We do not share your data with brand partners or any commercial third parties. We may share your data with:

  • Our secure data storage providers — only to the extent necessary to store and process your data securely
  • Our payment processing provider — limited to what is necessary to process your payment
  • Law enforcement or regulatory authorities — only where we are legally required to do so

Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — you may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — you may request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure — you may request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to restrict processing — you may request that we limit how we use your data
  • Right to data portability — you may request your data in a portable format
  • Right to object — you may object to certain types of processing
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time

To exercise any of these rights please contact us at hello@theskinsage.com. We will respond within 30 days. There is no charge for exercising your rights.

Complaints

If you are not satisfied with how we have handled your personal data you have the right to make a complaint to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) — the UK supervisory authority for data protection. You can contact the ICO at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

Cookies

Our website uses minimal cookies necessary for the website to function. We do not use advertising cookies or tracking cookies from third party advertisers.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any significant changes will be communicated to clients who have active consultations with us. The current version of this policy is always available at theskinsage.com/privacy-policy.html.

Contact us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your personal data please contact us at hello@theskinsage.com.
